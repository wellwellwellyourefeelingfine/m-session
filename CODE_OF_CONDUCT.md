# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or level of experience.

## Our Standards

**Positive behaviors include:**
- Being respectful and empathetic in all interactions
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get started
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project

**Unacceptable behaviors include:**
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Trolling, insulting remarks, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Topic-Specific Guidelines

This project involves a sensitive subject. We ask that all participants:

- **Approach discussions about substance use with respect and without judgment.** People come to this project from many backgrounds — harm reduction advocates, therapists, researchers, and individuals seeking safer practices.
- **Do not promote or encourage illegal activity.** This project is a harm reduction and therapeutic support tool. Discussions should stay focused on safety, well-being, and the therapeutic context.
- **Be sensitive to people in recovery or with trauma histories.** Language matters. Avoid glorifying or trivializing substance use.
- **Respect that harm reduction is a valid public health approach.** Disagreements about policy are fine; dismissing the value of harm reduction is not.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channels associated with this repository.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainers via GitHub issues or email. All reports will be reviewed and handled with discretion.

Project maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behaviors they deem inappropriate.
